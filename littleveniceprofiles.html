Carl Carl has been living on the canals for about ten years, he’s found the experience brilliant. Carl thinks boat-life is very peaceful. & It gives him a sense of freedom he couldn’t achieve living in brick and mortar.

In the one year I’ve been on the canal I’ve realised that it’s not all smooth sailing, you must expect the unexpected. It’s all a great learning experience but you must be very organised. When all is going well, you just can’t beat boat life! - Carl

Tess Tess has been living on narrow boats for about five years, she is currently living onboard the October with her partner and two young children. Tess has enjoyed life on the canals but worries, as her children grow older schooling might become an issue.

We love living on a boat, especially with kids! My two-year-old think houses (and flushing toilets) are a real novelty. He loves boat moving day and wants to drive the boat ‘just like Daddy’. We enjoy exploring our new neighbourhoods: finding the best playgrounds and play centre, re-discovering our favourite little shops and restaurants, and visiting local attractions. It’s a bit like going on holiday every two weeks! - Tess
