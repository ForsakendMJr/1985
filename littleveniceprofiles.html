Carl Carl has been living on the canals for about ten years, he’s found the experience brilliant. Carl thinks boat-life is very peaceful. & It gives him a sense of freedom he couldn’t achieve living in brick and mortar.

In the one year I’ve been on the canal I’ve realised that it’s not all smooth sailing, you must expect the unexpected. It’s all a great learning experience but you must be very organised. When all is going well, you just can’t beat boat life! - Carl

Tess Tess has been living on narrow boats for about five years, she is currently living onboard the October with her partner and two young children. Tess has enjoyed life on the canals but worries, as her children grow older schooling might become an issue.

We love living on a boat, especially with kids! My two-year-old think houses (and flushing toilets) are a real novelty. He loves boat moving day and wants to drive the boat ‘just like Daddy’. We enjoy exploring our new neighbourhoods: finding the best playgrounds and play centre, re-discovering our favourite little shops and restaurants, and visiting local attractions. It’s a bit like going on holiday every two weeks! - Tess

Nathan Nathan has been living on the canals with his wife for three years now. Nathan thinks life on the canals has its ups and downs, but it has been a mostly great experience for them. Nathan and his wife run a performing arts centre and work as freelance musicians and dancers.

I think a lot of people romanticise boat life, they think it’s going to be all smooth sailing. It’s important for them to understand what we really go through, however. I would implore those who want to try out the lifestyle to live on the canals by themselves for at least two weeks through the winter. You will see the complete truth of what it’s all about. - Nathan

Ken Ken has been living on his boat for the past seven years, he finds life on the canals peaceful. Ken works in catering and loves Indian food, he isn’t too fond of English food, however. He thinks life on the canals is a good cost-efficient alternative to renting accommodation in the city.

The seasons play a big part in your life when living on a narrowboat. The summertime is lovely, things get a bit bleak in the winter though. When the weather starts getting colder, I normally just shut my door and go to bed! - Ken

Karen and Tim Karen and Tim have been living aboard the ‘Maybe Knot’ for a little over eight and a half years. They have had a mostly pleasant experience on the canals. They both work in television and entertainment.

Almost nine years ago we got together and sold our house, to buy our narrow boat. This gave us the freedom to work in film and television without the pressure of the dreaded brown envelopes through the letterbox. In our experience as constant cruisers, we’ve found the majority of boaters very friendly and helpful without being nosey or clingy. Everyone values their privacy. Life is good afloat the boat that rocks. - Karen and Tim

Jasmine Jasmine has been living onboard ‘Simba’ for three years now. She spent one year renovating the boat from scratch, but now enjoys all the trappings of a small modern apartment. Jasmine works as a freelance filmmaker. Last year, she produced a film of her own.

Living on the canals is a million miles different to living on land, it isn’t for everyone. It’s much more physically demanding, but the experience is freeing. I love being connected to nature, experiencing the seasons as they ‘come and go’ is grounding. - Jasmine
